IMCI Study Module

Integrated Management of Childhood Illnesses

For sick children aged 2 months – 5 years

Epidemiology & Overview

Top Causes of Mortality

  • Newborns: Pneumonia (2/1,000 LB), Bacterial sepsis (1.8/1,000)
  • 0-4 years: Pneumonia (37.76/100k), Accidents (17.63/100k)
  • 5-9 years: Accidents, Pneumonia, Malignant neoplasms

Population Estimates

0-4 years: ~10 million children
5-10 years: ~10 million children
Newborns: first month of life

IMCI goal: Reduce morbidity & mortality in children <5 years.

General Danger Signs (ANY ONE = VERY SEVERE DISEASE)

🔴 Unable to drink/breastfeed – child cannot suck or swallow when offered.
🔴 Vomits everything – cannot hold down any food/fluids/medicine.
🔴 Convulsions – stiff arms/legs, loss of consciousness.
🔴 Lethargic or unconscious – unusually sleepy, hard to wake, or cannot be wakened.

Assessment steps: Ask problem → check danger signs → ask 4 main symptoms (cough, diarrhea, fever, ear) → check nutrition/immunization → assess other problems.

Color‑Coded Classification

🟢 GREEN
Mild → Home care
🟡 YELLOW
Moderate → RHU management
🔴 PINK
Severe → Urgent hospital referral

⚠️ If a child has 1 PINK + 1 YELLOW, treat as PINK (urgent referral). Exception: Dehydration requires 2 or more signs for severe classification.

Diarrhea & Dehydration Assessment

Dehydration signs (2 or more = severe)

  • Restless/irritable vs lethargic/unconscious
  • Sunken eyes
  • Drinks eagerly / poorly / not able
  • Skin pinch: slow or very slow (>2 sec)

Classification

  • Severe dehydration (pink) → IV fluids, urgent referral
  • Some dehydration (yellow) → Plan B (ORS + continue feeding)
  • No dehydration (green) → home care, extra fluids
📌 Persistent diarrhea ≥14 days → special feeding; refer if severe. Dysentery (blood in stool) → antibiotics + dehydration management.

Fever (≥37.5°C) & Measles

Fever >7 days → consider typhoid or severe disease. Check for stiff neck (meningitis → very severe).

Measles: generalized rash + cough/runny nose/red eyes. Complications: mouth ulcers, pus draining eyes, corneal clouding → give Vitamin A.

Malaria risk: high/ low/ no risk areas. Runny nose makes malaria unlikely in low/no risk areas.

Ear Problem

  • Tender swelling behind ear (mastoiditis) → URGENT referral, give antibiotic & paracetamol.
  • Pus draining <14 days or ear pain → acute ear infection → oral antibiotic 5 days + pain relief + dry ear.
  • Pus draining ≥14 days → chronic ear infection → dry ear only, follow-up 5 days.
  • No ear pain, no pus → no ear infection.

Malnutrition & Anemia

Look for: visible severe wasting (marasmus), palmar pallor (anemia), edema of both feet (kwashiorkor).
Measure weight for age: below bottom curve = very low weight.

Iron supplementation duration: 3 months; follow‑up at 14 days for pallor.

PHN Role in IMCI

  • Assess children for danger signs, fast breathing, chest indrawing, stridor.
  • Classify illness using color‑coded tables (green/yellow/pink).
  • Provide home care (ORS, antibiotics, feeding advice) for green/yellow.
  • Refer pink cases urgently; give pre‑referral treatment (first dose antibiotic, vitamin A).
  • Counsel caretakers on when to return, feeding during illness, and follow‑up schedules.
  • Maintain IMCI recording forms and coordinate with RHU/hospital.
